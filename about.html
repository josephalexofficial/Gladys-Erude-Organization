About The Gladys Erude Organization

Learn about our mission, vision, and the values that guide our work in the community.

Empowering Communities • Building Futures

What Inspires Us

Respected Kenyan journalist and community advocate, Gladys Adisa Erude, died in August 2021 after a battle with cancer. Gladys was born in Tigoi, Vihiga County, where she attended Tigoi Primary School and Lugulu Girls High School. She trained as a teacher before making her mark as a celebrated broadcaster.

Through GEO, her six sons seek to uphold their mother's dedication to children's education and women's empowerment, and to inspire others and mobilise support.

Today, we continue to honor Gladys's legacy by expanding our reach and deepening our impact, guided by her vision of a world where every individual has access to quality education and opportunities to thrive.

Our volunteers inspire change every day

Our Mission

By 2030, GEO aims to empower at least 10,000 women and children in underserved communities across Kenya by providing access to essential services such as education, healthcare, and economic opportunities.

Founded in 2022, GEO is a non-profit organization registered in the United States with active partnerships in Kenya, committed to creating measurable, lasting impact through sustainable, community-driven programs.

🎯
Target: 10,000+ Lives Impacted

Our Vision

A world where every individual has access to quality education, healthcare, and opportunities to reach their full potential, regardless of their background or circumstances.
